Biography

I’m currently pursuing a M.S. in Aerospace Engineering at the University of Arizona. I do research under Jekan Thanga within the Space and Terrestrial Robotic Exploration (SpaceTREx) Laboratory.

I am also a student researcher at the U.S. Naval Research Laboratory working in the Laboratory for Autonomous Systems Research (LASR). My research at the NRL includes design and control of Lighter than Air vehicles as well as swarming algorithms. LTA's provide an excellent platform for testing algorithms in a slow-moving and controlled environment. I was also a lead engineer for SWAT-C, a tactical capture the flag event that utilizes human robot teams. I was involved in developing automous control of a ground rover, a motion camera alert system, and an android app interface under the SWAT-C project.

While with CRaGL, I researched converting 2D shapes into cuttable furniture boards. The code and paper can be found here. I’ve also done research with the Air Force Research Lab and NASA. Links to the recent research I did with NASA can be found in the links below.

Aside from my studies and research, I am active in the outdoor community: skiing, backpacking, rock climbing, and camping. I’m also an avid adventure photographer. I currently have one album on Spotify and hope to release another in Fall of 2018.
